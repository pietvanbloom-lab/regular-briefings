Poolside ships an open-weight coding model small enough to run on a laptop

Tier 2 High Early signal Open Source and Research

On July 2, Poolside released Laguna XS 2.1, a 33 billion total and 3 billion active parameter mixture-of-experts coding model under the OpenMDW license with a 256K context window, alongside a larger 225 billion parameter sibling, Laguna M.1. It scores 70.9 percent on SWE-bench Verified and 47.6 percent on SWE-Bench Pro, runs on a Mac with 36GB of RAM, and ships with launch-day support in vLLM, SGLang, llama.cpp, Transformers, and TRT-LLM plus FP8, NVFP4, INT4, and GGUF quantizations. Free inference is offered temporarily on OpenRouter. Source: poolside.ai, huggingface.co

Why it matters: A 3 billion active parameter model that clears 70 percent on SWE-bench Verified and fits in laptop memory puts a capable agentic coder on-device, where no token bill and no data leaving the machine change the calculus for regulated and cost-sensitive teams. The falsifiable test is adoption in tools, if a mainstream coding assistant ships a local Laguna-class model as a selectable backend within two quarters, on-device coding stops being a hobbyist setup. Watch the gap between the open 33B and frontier hosted coders, because if small open weights keep closing on SWE-bench, the premium buyers will pay for a hosted coding model shrinks to latency and orchestration.
