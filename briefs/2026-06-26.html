OpenAI and Broadcom unveil Jalapeño, OpenAI's first custom inference chip

Tier 1 High Frontier Labs / Data Centers

OpenAI revealed Jalapeño, an ASIC co-designed with Broadcom and built from scratch for LLM inference rather than adapted from a training accelerator. OpenAI says it went from design to tape-out in about nine months, used its own models to speed parts of the work, and expects performance per watt well above current state of the art, with first deployment targeted for the end of 2026. Announced late June 24 US time, with the coverage wave running into June 25. Source: openai.com Jalapeno, cnbc.com

Why it matters: This is OpenAI moving from buying compute to owning the silicon roadmap, and an inference-first ASIC is aimed squarely at the part of the cost curve that actually scales with ChatGPT usage, not at training headlines. The falsifiable test is the late-2026 deployment: if Jalapeño racks come online at gigawatt scale on schedule, it pressures Nvidia's inference margin and validates the custom-ASIC path that Google (TPU) and Amazon (Trainium) already walk. Watch two tells, whether Broadcom raises its AI revenue guide on the back of this program, and whether OpenAI discloses a second-generation part, because a multi-generation cadence is what separates a vanity chip from a real supply-chain hedge.

General Intuition raises $320M Series A at $2.3B to train action models on gameplay

Tier 1 High Early signal AI Business

The spatial-AI lab closed a $320M Series A at a $2.3B valuation, led by Khosla Ventures with General Catalyst, Hillspire and Jeff Bezos participating. It trains large action and world foundation models on billions of action-labeled, first-person gameplay clips from its Medal platform's roughly 17M monthly users, aiming at agents that act across space and time rather than generate text. Source: techcrunch.com, theaiinsider.tech

Why it matters: This is a concrete bet that the next data moat is not text scraped from the web but action-labeled experience, and gameplay is the cheapest large source of it. The early-signal angle is that a $2.3B valuation on a pre-product action-model lab tells you where frontier capital thinks embodied and agentic AI is heading next, ahead of any shipped robot or agent. Watch whether General Intuition publishes a benchmark on a real robotics or agent task, because the whole thesis is falsifiable the moment gameplay-trained policies either do or do not transfer to physical or tool-use environments.

OpenThoughts-Agent ships a fully open data recipe and 32B model that beats prior open-data agents

Tier 1 High Early signal Open Source / Research

A community effort released the OpenThoughts-Agent dataset, full pipeline, and OpenThinkerAgent-32B, the product of 100-plus ablations on agentic data curation. Fine-tuning Qwen3-32B on the 100K-example open set reaches 44.8% average across seven agentic benchmarks, about 3.9 points over the prior best open-data agent, with data, pipeline and weights all released. Source: openthoughts.ai, huggingface.co

Why it matters: The gap between closed agent models and what anyone can reproduce narrows fastest when the data recipe, not just the weights, is open, and that is exactly what shipped here. The falsifiable claim is the 3.9-point margin over Nemotron-Terminal-32B on a public seven-benchmark suite, so it is checkable rather than a vibe. Watch whether other open-weight labs adopt the curation pipeline and whether the next open-data agent jumps again, because a fast follow would mean the open agent stack is compounding rather than plateauing.
