WebMCP enters Chrome 149 origin trial: Google and Microsoft engineers ship browser-native MCP standard

Tier 1 High confidence Early signal Agentic / MCP

Two surface APIs ship: Declarative (HTML form actions) and Imperative (JS for dynamic interactions), letting sites expose structured MCP tools to browser agents instead of relying on visual scraping.

Source: developer.chrome.com / WebMCP early preview and venturebeat.com / Chrome WebMCP

Why this matters before others see it: Browser-side MCP collapses the distinction between "agent uses a tool" and "agent uses a website". Three implications to watch: (1) every site that adopts WebMCP becomes natively addressable by Claude, ChatGPT, Comet, and Antigravity agents without scraping or DOM heuristics, which kills a chunk of the AI scraping market overnight, (2) publishers and SaaS vendors get a new policy lever (rate limits, auth, opt-out) that the visual-scraping era never gave them, (3) Microsoft co-authorship signals Edge will follow fast, making this the first standard to ship across both major Chromium browsers before Apple Safari has a position.
