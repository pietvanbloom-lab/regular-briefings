Top 3 today

Anthropic brings Claude Cowork to web and mobile, and its own usage data shows most sessions are not coding

Tier 1HighAnthropic

On July 7 Anthropic moved Cowork off desktop-only onto web and mobile, with sessions, files, and remote runs that sync to a Claude account and continue after the laptop is closed. Rollout starts with the Max plan over several weeks. Alongside it, Anthropic published data from about 1.2 million anonymized Cowork sessions: business process and operations made up 33.4 percent of sessions, slides, docs, and proposals 16.4 percent, and software development just 8.7 percent. The company framed Cowork as tooling for "the work around work." Source: claude.com, techcrunch.com, venturebeat.com

Why it matters: Anthropic is pushing its agent past coding into the general knowledge-work market where it competes with Microsoft Copilot and OpenAI, and the 8.7 percent coding share is a concrete signal of where an agent actually gets used. Watch whether the non-coding mix holds as Cowork reaches Pro and Team plans, and whether rivals cite similar usage splits.

Meta's plan to resell spare AI compute sends CoreWeave and Nebius down about 15 percent

Tier 1HighAI Data Centers

Fresh July 7 analysis of Meta's "Meta Compute" plan, selling excess AI capacity to outside customers, weighed on the neocloud names: CoreWeave and Nebius fell roughly 15 percent intraday while Meta rose more than 10 percent. The structural worry is customer concentration, since Nebius holds a reported 27 billion dollar Meta arrangement and CoreWeave a reported 21 billion dollar one, so a key buyer turning into a competing seller reprices the risk. Source: fool.com

Why it matters: If hyperscalers start reselling their own capacity, the neocloud model of leasing GPUs to the same giants looks more fragile. Watch whether Meta publishes Compute pricing or named customers, and whether CoreWeave or Nebius disclose new non-hyperscaler demand to offset the concentration read.

Palantir signs GNP Seguros, its first publicly named commercial customer in Latin America

Tier 1HighAI Business

On July 7 Palantir announced an agreement to scale Foundry and its AIP platform across GNP Seguros, one of Mexico's largest insurers, spanning fraud detection, underwriting, and claims across health, life, motor, and property lines. It is Palantir's first publicly announced commercial customer in the region, and the stock ticked higher on the release. Source: businesswire.com

Why it matters: A marquee Latin American logo gives Palantir a reference account to sell the rest of the region's insurers and banks on agentic underwriting and claims. Watch whether more Mexican or Brazilian financial firms follow within the quarter, which would mark a real regional beachhead rather than a one-off.
