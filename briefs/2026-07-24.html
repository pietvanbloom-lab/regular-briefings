White House accuses Moonshot of distilling Anthropic's Fable to build Kimi K3, and of routing Nvidia GB300 chips through Thailand

White House OSTP director Michael Kratsios said publicly on July 22, via a post on X, that Moonshot AI distilled Anthropic's Fable model to develop Kimi K3, calling it covert industrial distillation, and separately alleged Moonshot accessed export-restricted Nvidia GB300 servers in Thailand. It is the first time a senior US official has named a specific Chinese lab for copying a specific American model. Redwood Research chief scientist Ryan Greenblatt published a cross-entropy analysis finding K3 identifies as Claude at a rate hard to explain as noise. Moonshot has not conceded the claim and no legal finding exists. Source: aol.com, glitchwire.com

Why it matters: This lands four days before Kimi K3's open weights go free on July 27, so every enterprise planning to self-host now weighs a contested provenance claim on top of a technical evaluation, and the export allegation is the more legally concrete of the two. Watch whether Moonshot answers directly, whether the US frontier AI framework (expected before August 1) tightens cloud-access and know-your-customer rules for compute, and whether enterprise adoption of Chinese open weights slows while individual developers keep moving.
Tier 1AI Data CentersHigh impact

OpenAI commits to Project Camellia, a 3.2 gigawatt Georgia data center campus

OpenAI announced Project Camellia on July 22, a 3.2 gigawatt AI data center campus on about 1,400 acres in Effingham County, Georgia. Georgia Power will deliver electricity in phases from 2028 through 2032, and OpenAI says it will fully fund the required grid infrastructure so existing ratepayers do not subsidize the project, using closed-loop cooling to limit water use. Reported spend figures vary, from about 20 billion dollars (Axios) to more than 30 billion (secondary coverage). Source: prnewswire.com, axios.com

Why it matters: 3.2 gigawatts is roughly three large nuclear reactors dedicated to one campus, and power arriving in 2028 to 2032 confirms that electricity, not chips, sets the AI competitive timeline. The commitments to fund grid infrastructure and use closed-loop cooling directly answer the ratepayer-subsidy and water objections driving local opposition. Watch whether those pledges survive construction cost pressure, and whether rivals (Meta at 14 gigawatts, others) match the self-funded grid model.
Tier 1Anthropic / ClaudeMedium impact

Anthropic brings Opus and Sonnet to Claude voice mode with cross-app automation

Anthropic upgraded Claude voice mode on July 23 to run on Opus and Sonnet for the first time, not just the lightweight Haiku model. Voice now defaults to the last model picked in text chat, allows switching mid-conversation, and adds cross-app automation with Gmail, Google Calendar, Slack, Canva, and Notion across ten languages. Free users get Haiku and single-app use, paid tiers unlock all models and multi-app actions. Source: techcrunch.com, 9to5mac.com

Why it matters: Voice moves from a fast-but-shallow Haiku demo to a reasoning-capable assistant that can take actions across the apps where work already happens, which is the same agent-plus-permissions pattern the enterprise platforms are racing to own. Watch whether latency holds when Opus handles voice turns, whether the cross-app actions get real guardrails and audit, and whether OpenAI and Google answer on their own voice tiers.
