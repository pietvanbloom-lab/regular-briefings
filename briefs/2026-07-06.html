Top 3 today

OpenAI floats giving the US government a 5 percent stake in itself, and asks rivals to match

Tier 2 reportedHighAI Business

The Financial Times reported on July 2, picked up by Bloomberg and CNBC, that Sam Altman and other OpenAI executives have opened preliminary talks to hand Washington a 5 percent equity stake, worth about 42.6 billion dollars at the company's 852 billion dollar valuation. The pitch goes further: Altman proposed the government hold 5 percent of every leading US lab, potentially Anthropic, Google, and Meta, pooled in an Alaska Permanent Fund style vehicle so the public shares AI's upside. The talks are described as conceptual and could require an act of Congress, and it is unclear whether the other named firms would agree. Source: bloomberg.com, cnbc.com

Why it matters: Equity would fuse the roles of regulator and shareholder for the frontier labs. Watch whether Anthropic and Google respond, and whether Congress engages, since the pitch lands the same week the White House is finalizing its frontier-model release framework.

Meta's plan to resell AI compute keeps routing the neoclouds, CoreWeave and Nebius slide into the weekend

Tier 1Med-HighAI Data Centers

Fallout from Meta's July 1 plan to rent out spare AI compute continued through July 3 to 5: CoreWeave fell roughly 14 percent to about 85.69 dollars and Nebius dropped roughly 12 to 15 percent, on fears Meta shifts from a major customer to an AWS-style competitor. The move spotlights customer-concentration risk across the neocloud sector, where Nebius holds a reported 27 billion dollar Meta agreement and CoreWeave a 21 billion dollar deal. It is the first clear market verdict on whether hyperscalers reselling capacity undercuts the specialist GPU clouds. Source: fool.com

Why it matters: The selloff prices in a real structural risk. Watch CoreWeave's 21 billion and Nebius's 27 billion dollar Meta commitments for any renegotiation or contract-status signal, and whether other anchor customers follow Meta into self-supply.

Abu Dhabi's MGX closes a record 49 billion dollar AI fund, 4 billion over target

Tier 1HighAI Business

MGX closed its first fund on July 1 at 49 billion dollars, above a 45 billion dollar target, making it one of the largest dedicated AI investment vehicles yet, spanning semiconductors, infrastructure, and platforms. The fund has already backed 14 companies and is a significant investor in the labs it may now steer capital toward: MGX co-led Anthropic's February raise, took part in its May Series H, co-led OpenAI's March round, and joined xAI's January raise. Reaching its stated goal of more than 100 billion dollars in assets will require deploying up to 10 billion dollars a year. Source: cnbc.com, thenationalnews.com

Why it matters: A 49 billion dollar vehicle deploying up to 10 billion a year becomes a swing buyer across chips, infrastructure, and models. Watch where MGX writes its next checks, and how Gulf sovereign capital sits alongside a possible US government equity stake in the same labs.
