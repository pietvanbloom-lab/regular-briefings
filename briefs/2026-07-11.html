MCP: release candidate for the stateless spec is out ahead of the July 28 final

The Model Context Protocol release candidate for the 2026 revision is published, the largest change since launch: a stateless core that scales on ordinary HTTP, MCP Apps for server-rendered UIs, a Tasks extension for long-running work, OAuth and OpenID-aligned auth, and a formal deprecation policy. Separately, the Enterprise-Managed Authorization extension was promoted to stable, with Anthropic (Claude, Claude Code, Cowork) and VS Code implementing it and Asana, Atlassian, Canva, Figma, Granola, Linear and Supabase listed as supporting servers. Source: modelcontextprotocol.io, infoq.com

Google DeepMind pushed Gemini 3.5 Pro to July 17, scrapping the 2.5 Pro architecture for a complete rebuild targeting mathematical reasoning, SVG scene generation and image quality, positioned against GPT-5.6 and Fable 5. The delay is now the near-term calendar item to watch after a week where every other major lab shipped. Source: biggo.com

AI Safety Index: nobody scores above C+, Anthropic tops the table

The Future of Life Institute's 2026 first-half AI Safety Index (released July 7) graded nine labs and gave none an A or B. Anthropic ranked first at C+, leading five of six domains on transparency, framework maturity, technical research and governance, followed by OpenAI and Google DeepMind at C, Meta at D+, and xAI at F. The panel flagged weakened voluntary commitments across the board, including Anthropic dropping a prior training-safety pledge in February and reversals on military-use restrictions. Source: time.com, govinfosecurity.com

OpenAI's deployment arm to acquire Northslope, its second applied-AI buy since May

OpenAI Deployment Company, the majority-owned services arm launched in May with about $4bn to fund acquisitions, agreed to acquire Palantir-rooted Northslope (reported July 8), adding hundreds of forward-deployed engineers who embed inside customer organizations to build AI systems. Northslope grew revenue roughly 7x in 2025. The move mirrors Palantir's embed-engineers playbook and signals OpenAI is buying enterprise adoption capacity, not just selling models. Source: axios.com

ByteDance Seedream 5.0 Pro: a reasoning image model with layer editing and 2K output

Detail from the July 8 to 9 launch now settling: Seedream 5.0 Pro adds deep-thinking prompt reasoning, real-time web search, interactive precision editing (point, lasso, sketch, color and material swaps), layer separation to alpha PNGs, native 2K output, and on-image text in 10-plus languages. It rolled out first on ByteDance's Volcano Engine and expanded to BytePlus, Doubao, Jimeng, and third-party hosts including fal and ComfyUI. Source: seed.bytedance.com, testingcatalog.com

OpenAI GPT-Live rolls out globally: full-duplex voice that delegates to a frontier model

Also from July 8 and worth flagging for the weekend reader: GPT-Live-1 and GPT-Live-1 mini began rolling out to ChatGPT users globally across iOS, Android and web, powering ChatGPT Voice with a full-duplex architecture that listens and speaks at once, offers backchannel cues, and hands off to a frontier model mid-conversation for web search or deeper reasoning. It runs on GPT-5.5 in the background at launch, with API access flagged as coming soon. Source: openai.com, techcrunch.com
