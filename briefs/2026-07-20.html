MCP's Enterprise-Managed Authorization extension reaches stable

The Model Context Protocol team promoted its Enterprise-Managed Authorization extension to stable status, giving organizations a centralized way to control access to MCP servers through their identity provider. Eight days to the MCP 2026 stateless final: routing headers are the sleeper change

The 2026-07-28 specification remains on track, making MCP stateless at the protocol layer with state moved into visible handles and capabilities into negotiated extensions. A practical detail drawing attention this weekend: Streamable HTTP now requires Mcp-Method and Mcp-Name headers, letting load balancers and rate-limiters route on the operation without inspecting the body. Open Source and Research

Epoch AI: leading text detectors miss up to 18 percent of style-imitating AI output

Epoch AI tested Pangram, GPTZero, and Originality.ai against text generated in imitation of a specific author's style and found up to 18 percent of AI passages went undetected, with scientific writing the weakest spot. Detection is losing an asymmetric race: style imitation costs a prompt, catching it is a hard statistical problem. Source: buildfastwithai.com

RadLE 2.0 benchmark: radiology models are confidently wrong

A new benchmark called RadLE 2.0 found AI models frequently deliver incorrect radiology findings with full confidence and no uncertainty signal. The miscalibration matters more than the raw error rate, because confident wrong answers defeat the human review that is supposed to catch them, a caution for the wave of health AI deployments funded this month.
