DeepSeek publishes the DeepSeek-V4 technical report (arXiv 2606.19348), a 1.6T-parameter MoE built for million-token context with hybrid compressed attention that cuts long-context inference cost sharply

Tier 1 High confidence Early signal

The report describes two mixture-of-experts models: V4-Pro at 1.6T total parameters with 49B active per token, and V4-Flash at 284B total with 13B active, both with a 1M-token context. The attention stack interleaves two compression strategies across layers, Compressed Sparse Attention and a more aggressive dense-compression variant, on top of DeepSeek Sparse Attention. The headline efficiency claim: at 1M-token context, V4-Pro uses about 27 percent of the per-token inference FLOPs and about 10 percent of the KV cache versus DeepSeek-V3.2. Training used the Muon optimizer and a distillation pipeline that merges ten teacher models into one, and weights are staged on Hugging Face under deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro.

Source: arxiv.org DeepSeek-V4 technical report, huggingface.co DeepSeek-V4-Pro

Why it matters: The binding cost of million-token context is not the parameter count, it is the attention compute and the KV cache, and DeepSeek is publishing a recipe that attacks both at once rather than just widening the window. If the 27 percent FLOPs and 10 percent KV-cache numbers hold in independent testing, the marginal cost of serving very long context drops enough to change which agentic and document workloads are economic to run. Watch whether the hybrid attention design gets copied into the next open-weight releases from Qwen and Z.ai, and whether US labs answer with their own long-context efficiency papers rather than ceding the cost frontier.

Anthropic ships enterprise-managed authorization for MCP connectors with Okta as first identity provider, so admins provision connectors org-wide and users inherit them on first login

Tier 1 High confidence Early signal

The Enterprise-Managed Authorization (EMA) extension to the Model Context Protocol lets an admin centrally authorize MCP connectors through the company identity provider, with users auto-provisioned on first login across Claude chat, Claude Code, and Cowork. Seven servers support it at launch: Asana, Atlassian, Canva, Figma, Granola, Linear, and Supabase, with Slack in progress and HubSpot, Ramp, and Webflow named as early adopters. It is in beta for Team and Enterprise plans, Okta is the only identity provider at launch, and Azure AD and Google Workspace are on the roadmap with no date.

Source: claude.com Centrally manage MCP authorization, okta.com featured identity provider for Claude

Why it matters: MCP has been a developer-tinkering layer, and identity-provider governance is the step that lets a CISO say yes to it at scale. Auto-provisioning connectors on login removes the per-user OAuth dance that has slowed enterprise rollout, and it quietly makes the identity provider, not the app, the control point for which agents can touch which systems. Watch whether OpenAI and Google match IdP-managed connector auth, and whether the seven launch servers become the de facto enterprise MCP shortlist that later entrants must join to be taken seriously.

Nvidia-backed Verse raises a 54M USD Series B and launches Dispatch Intelligence, software that orchestrates on-site power so AI data centers can come online up to three years faster

Tier 1 High confidence Early signal

San Francisco-based Verse closed an oversubscribed 54M USD round led by Bessemer Venture Partners, with GV, NVIDIA, and Norrsken VC participating. Dispatch Intelligence, built with energy storage firm Calibrant Energy, orchestrates on-site batteries and solar so a data center reduces its draw on the grid during peak periods, which the partners say can pull interconnection approvals forward by years. Verse is integrating the product with Nvidia's DSX AI Factory reference design for gigascale sites.

Source: globenewswire.com Verse Dispatch Intelligence, datacenterdynamics.com Verse Series B

Why it matters: The constraint on the 2026 to 2027 AI buildout is no longer chips, it is grid interconnection, and the queue to connect a large load now runs years. Verse turns flexibility into software: if a site can promise the grid it will throttle on demand, it can skip ahead in the interconnection queue. That reframes speed-to-power as a controls problem rather than a transmission problem, and Nvidia putting it inside the DSX reference design signals it wants this baked into how gigascale sites are designed. Watch whether utilities formally credit software-defined flexible load in interconnection rules, which would turn this from a pitch into a recognized fast lane.
