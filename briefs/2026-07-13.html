Tier 1Frontier / LegalHigh impact

Apple sues OpenAI, alleging trade-secret theft tied to its hardware push

Apple filed a 41-page complaint in the Northern District of California on July 10 naming OpenAI, its hardware unit io Products, hardware chief Tang Tan, and a former Apple engineer, alleging a coordinated effort to lift product designs, manufacturing processes, and supply-chain strategy. Apple says more than 400 former staff now work at OpenAI and is seeking damages and an injunction. Source: cnn.com

Why it matters: An injunction could stall OpenAI's unreleased AI-device roadmap, and the case would be the first big test of policing the lab-to-lab talent poaching that powers the whole industry's hiring engine.
Tier 1Data Centers / FinancingHigh impact

Big Tech's AI debt load reaches about $350 billion as AI-backed bond issuance passes $250 billion

Bloomberg reported on July 10 that the five biggest AI spenders (Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Microsoft, Oracle) have roughly doubled combined debt over five years, adding about $350 billion, to fund data-center buildouts. July alone featured a $25 billion Amazon bond package, with operator QTS marketing another $2 billion; total bond issuance backing AI investment has now passed $250 billion. Source: bloomberg.com, finance.yahoo.com

Why it matters: Capex is shifting from cash-funded to debt-funded, so credit spreads on hyperscaler and neocloud paper become the earliest read on when the buildout slows.
