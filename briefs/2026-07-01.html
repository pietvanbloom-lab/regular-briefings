US Commerce lifts export controls on Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5, access returns

Tier 1 High AI Business

Anthropic confirmed on June 30 that the Bureau of Industry and Security withdrew the export controls placed on Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5, and began restoring access on July 1. The original June 12 directive, three days after the models went live on June 9, cited national-security concerns tied to a jailbreak risk and had forced Anthropic to cut access for all foreign nationals inside and outside the US. Anthropic says it worked with the government on a new safeguard that blocks the flagged jailbreak about 93 percent of the time, and several outlets read the clearance as removing an overhang ahead of a possible IPO. Source: cnbc.com, axios.com, foxbusiness.com

Why it matters: A frontier lab had two live models pulled offline by the government for three weeks over a jailbreak, then restored them by negotiating a safeguard commitment, which sets a template for how model-level export enforcement now works. The falsifiable read is whether this becomes routine: if BIS applies the same release-then-restrict-then-safeguard pattern to another lab's model this year, pre-clearance security review becomes a standing gate on frontier launches. Watch the safeguard reporting cadence Anthropic agreed to, because a recurring obligation to detect and report misuse is a compliance cost that scales with every future release.

DeepSeek dates V4 for mid-July and publishes a peak-hour API pricing table

Tier 1 High Frontier LLM Labs

DeepSeek told API users on June 30 that the official V4 release lands in mid-July and, for the first time, introduces time-of-day pricing. Rates for V4 Pro and V4 Flash stay flat off-peak but roughly double during peak windows of 9:00 to 12:00 and 14:00 to 18:00, with 24 hours notice before any change. V4 Pro peak output runs 12 RMB per million tokens against 6 RMB off-peak, and V4 Flash peak output runs 4 RMB against 2 RMB. Source: technode.com, pandaily.com

Why it matters: Time-of-day pricing treats inference like an electricity grid, admitting that GPU capacity, not model quality, is now the binding constraint on a low-cost provider. The falsifiable test is imitation: if any Western lab introduces peak and off-peak API tiers within two quarters, demand-shaping pricing becomes an industry tool rather than a DeepSeek quirk. Watch whether enterprises start scheduling batch and agent workloads into off-peak windows, because load that moves to chase price is the signal that compute scarcity is reaching the buyer, not just the provider.
