Top 3 today

OpenAI clears GPT-5.6 for a global rollout, with the public launch set for July 9

Tier 1HighFrontier LLM

On July 8 OpenAI said a government-requested limited preview restriction had been lifted, so it is expanding GPT-5.6 access worldwide and setting the full public launch for Thursday July 9. The family has three tiers: Sol, the strongest, priced at 5 and 30 dollars per million input and output tokens, Terra, matching GPT-5.5 quality at roughly half the cost, and Luna, the cheapest. Reporting around the rollout puts Sol at 88.8 percent on Terminal-Bench 2.1, ahead of GPT-5.5, with a Cerebras option quoted near 750 tokens per second. Source: openai.com, cnbc.com, nextgov.com

Why it matters: A frontier model going from gated preview to global availability resets the price and capability bar that every rival now has to answer, and the government-review step is itself a signal of how the biggest launches will be paced. Watch whether OpenAI holds the July 9 date, whether Sol pricing sticks after launch demand, and how fast Google and Anthropic respond.

Microsoft starts routing Excel and Outlook AI prompts to its own MAI models to pay OpenAI and Anthropic less

Tier 1HighAI Business

A wave of July 8 coverage followed a Bloomberg report that Microsoft has begun completing tens of thousands of weekly Copilot prompts in Excel and Outlook with its in-house MAI models rather than OpenAI and Anthropic. MAI chief Mustafa Suleyman framed it as reducing, and eventually eliminating, what Microsoft pays Anthropic, and Microsoft says its Excel-tuned model matches GPT-5.4 accuracy while running about ten times more efficiently. Microsoft stock rose around 2 percent. Source: bloomberg.com, techtimes.com

Why it matters: The largest single distributor of frontier AI is starting to backward-integrate away from its suppliers, which pressures the per-seat economics OpenAI and Anthropic count on. Watch which apps move to MAI next, whether Microsoft publishes quality parity data, and how the Anthropic and OpenAI relationships are renegotiated.

xAI ships Grok 4.5, an Opus-class model it says was co-trained with Cursor

Tier 1HighFrontier LLM

On July 8 xAI released Grok 4.5, its strongest model yet, aimed at coding, agentic, and knowledge work and described by Elon Musk as Opus-class. It was trained alongside Cursor and is available in Grok Build, in Cursor, and via the console, priced at 2 and 6 dollars per million input and output tokens with a claimed 2x token efficiency gain. EU availability is delayed to mid-July. Source: x.ai, techcrunch.com

Why it matters: Two frontier labs shipping strong coding models on the same day tightens the race, and co-training with a coding-tool vendor is a distribution play that puts xAI directly in developers' editors. Watch independent benchmarks against GPT-5.6 Sol and Claude, whether the Cursor tie-in drives real seat share, and how the mid-July EU gap plays out.
