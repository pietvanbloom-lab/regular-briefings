Apple rebuilds Siri as a standalone "Siri AI" app powered by a custom Google Gemini model, under a deal reported near 1 billion dollars a year

Tier 1 High confidence Competitor

At Tim Cook's June 8 WWDC keynote Apple previewed Siri AI, a rebuilt assistant running on a custom Google Gemini model under a multi-year licensing deal reported around 1 billion dollars a year. Siri AI ships as a standalone app on iPhone, iPad, and Mac with persistent synced history, multi-step and cross-app commands, and image and document attachments, using a three-tier router that keeps simple tasks on device, sends moderate ones to Apple Private Cloud Compute, and routes the heaviest reasoning to Google Cloud with queries anonymized. iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 also let users set a third-party model such as Claude or ChatGPT as the default for Apple Intelligence features like Writing Tools and Image Playground. Siri AI does not ship in the EU or China at launch.

Source: apple.com newsroom, TechCrunch, CNBC, first seen 2026-06-08

The third-party default toggle turns Apple Intelligence into a routing layer rather than a model, so the real fight becomes which provider a billion users pick by default. Watch the EU and China exclusions become an opening for local and sovereign models, and watch Anthropic push Claude as the privacy-first default inside that same toggle.

An unreleased Anthropic frontier model, "Claude Mythos Preview," reaches Hitachi as it joins Project Glasswing for offensive-grade vulnerability finding

Tier 2 High confidence Early signal Anthropic

Hitachi (TSE:6501) said on June 8 it has joined Anthropic's Project Glasswing cybersecurity program and will gain access to Claude Mythos Preview, an unreleased general-purpose frontier model.
